# HyperFrames iOS Review Design

## Style Prompt

A calm, evidence-first release review for an iOS memory-training app. The composition should feel like a family care product being prepared for App Store release: precise, legible, warm, and restrained. Use a light cyan paper field, deep teal text, amber emphasis for memory cues, and quiet water-ripple motifs. The mood is not promotional; it is a clear executive handoff.

## Colors

- Background: `#ECFEFF`
- Foreground: `#164E63`
- Surface: `#FFFFFF`
- Primary: `#0891B2`
- Accent: `#D97706`
- Danger: `#DC2626`
- Muted: `#64748B`
- Border: `#A5F3FC`

## Typography

- Display: `Fraunces`, Georgia, serif
- Body: `Aptos`, `-apple-system`, sans-serif
- Data: `Roboto Mono`, monospace

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use purple AI gradients or neon tech styling.
- Do not use emoji as structural icons.
- Do not make every scene a uniform card grid.
- Do not imply iOS release readiness; distinguish document completion from release completion.
- Do not hide blockers behind optimistic language.
